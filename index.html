Paul Walker

Here's a time line of Paul Walker's life:

  • 1973 - Paul William Walker IV was born on September 12, in Glendale, California. He was the eldest of five children.
  • 1986 - Walker started his career in front of the camera at an early age, starring in a television commercial for Pampers.
  • 1991 - He graduated from Village Christian School in Sun Valley, California.
  • 1998 - Walker's breakthrough role came when he played a supporting role in the film "Pleasantville," which gained him recognition in Hollywood.
  • 2001 - Walker's career-defining role came when he starred as Brian O'Conner in "The Fast and the Furious," which became a massive success and established him as a leading actor in action films.
  • 2005 - Walker founded the charity "Reach Out Worldwide" (ROWW), which provides aid to regions affected by natural disasters.
  • 2013 - On November 30, 2013, Paul Walker tragically died in a car accident in Santa Clarita, California, at the age of 40. He was attending a charity event for his organization ROWW at the time.
  • 2015 - "Furious 7," the last film he completed before his death, was released. The film included a tribute to Walker and used CGI and his brothers, Caleb and Cody, as stand-ins to complete his remaining scenes.
  • 2017 - A documentary titled "I Am Paul Walker" was released, celebrating his life and legacy. It featured interviews with his family, friends, and colleagues, providing an intimate look at the man behind the action star.
